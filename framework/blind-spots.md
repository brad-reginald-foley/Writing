# Blind spots & common shortfalls — the watchlist

*The agent applies this to **every chapter and every idea/arc.** The universal craft shortfalls are
below; the **author's own** recurring tendencies are tracked separately and privately (in
`_private/blind-spots-personal.md`, gitignored). The job is to catch these BEFORE they cost the
writer — name them plainly and propose the fix.*

## Common craft shortfalls (check against these)
- **Self-insert / Mary Sue** — unearned competence, no real flaws, author wish-fulfillment.
  *Especially dangerous where the author over-identifies.*
- **Info-dump worldbuilding** — research delivered as exposition, not filtered through character/
  action. *Cut anything not load-bearing for character/theme/plot.*
- **Exposition-as-dialogue** ("As you know, Bob…").
- **On-the-nose theme** — characters stating the moral; theme without tension.
- **Passive protagonist** — things happen *to* them; no wants, no consequential choices.
  *(Active-and-wrong beats passive.)*
- **Sagging middle** — no try/fail escalation; the engine stalls.
- **Deus ex machina / plot convenience** — problems solved by luck or author fiat.
- **Unearned ending** — a change/victory the story didn't pay for.
- **Telling not showing** — incl. over-filtering ("she noticed / felt / saw").
- **Monoculture / stereotype** — a people/place/faction written as a bloc (*especially fraught for
  real cultures*; give every group internal variety, heroes and monsters).
- **Pacing front-load** — atmosphere before stakes; a scene that doesn't turn a charged value.
- **Characters as theme-mouthpieces** — positions, not people.
- **Subtlety miscalibration** — a clue too faint to land (or too loud). Decide *now-clue* vs
  *later-payoff* on purpose.
- **Research / structure as procrastination** — building the machine instead of writing the book.

## Author watch-fors (track the writer's own — privately)
*Beyond the universal shortfalls above, the agent keeps a short, honest, evolving list of **this
author's** recurring tendencies — the protagonist type they default to, the theme they keep
circling, the craft tic they repeat — i.e. the specific places to point the lenses HARDEST. Those
are personal, so they live in **`_private/blind-spots-personal.md`** (gitignored), not here. Start
one per author and keep it current.*

## How to use it
On each chapter (in the review) and each new idea/arc, run this as a checklist. When something
trips, **name it, locate it, and propose the fix.** When the **author's** instinct trips a watch-for,
**say so and push back** — that's the job.
