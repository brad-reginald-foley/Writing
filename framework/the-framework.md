# The framework — how we develop a novel

*Distilled from a working novel project (kept privately). This is a **diagnostic scaffold, not a formula** — develop in any order, skip what doesn't apply, and never force-fit. The point is to make sure nothing obvious gets missed.*

## The layers (roughly in order, but iterate freely)

### 1. Seed → logline
The "what if." One sentence. The **hook** that makes it worth writing. (Lives in `ideas.md` until it grows; then `premise.md`.)

### 2. Core question / thesis (the theme)
The **argument** the book is making — phrased as a tension, not a moral.
- **Keep it uncomfortable / non-facile.** Name the trite reading and refuse it. Find the claim that *stays* hard. (e.g. "capacity is real and unequal — and worth is still not capacity." Not "be yourself," not "genes are destiny.")
- Identify the **central tension** that won't resolve cleanly. That irresolution is the engine.

### 3. Characters
For each major character:
- **Want** (conscious, external goal) · **Need** (internal lack) · **the Lie** they believe (the engine between Want and Need).
- An **inner contradiction** (wants two incompatible things).
- **Arc** (how the Lie breaks, or doesn't) · key **relationships**.
- The cast as an **opposition web:** each major character embodies a *distinct stance on the theme*. The book is an argument; the characters are its positions.
- Names are **placeholders** until they earn themselves (track in `naming.md`).

### 4. Setting / worldbuilding
- The world's **rules** and institutions. Ground them (research real analogues).
- **Load-bearing only:** if a piece of world doesn't serve character/theme/plot, it's decoration — flag it.
- **Apparent vs. real:** what the world *seems* to be vs. what it *is* (a reliable engine for dread and reveals).

### 5. Arc / structure
- The **spine:** acts/movements; the key **turns** (each should flip a charged value).
- The **ending** — earn it; let it be as uncomfortable as the theme demands. Avoid the clean win.
- **Reader-knowledge design:** decide the gap between what the reader knows and what the POV character knows (small = mystery/discovery; large = tragic irony/dread). This is a *tunable knob* — track it.

### 6. Diagnostic pass (the craft lenses)
Run `craft-lenses.md` over the material to find blind spots — Want/Need/Lie, "does the scene turn a charged value?", causality (therefore/but not and-then), MICE (which thread frames the book), try/fail escalation, the reader-knowledge map. **Lenses talk back** — when an answer surprises you, it found something.

### 7. Drafting pipeline (only once you're writing chapters)
Add: `chapters.md` (index/manifest), `timeline.md`, `continuity.md` (canon + reader-knowledge
+ who-knows-what-when), per-chapter `.meta.md` files, and the **per-chapter review routine**
(read → honest review → meta → index canon → continuity-check → timeline → commit). Prose in `.md`; **assistant never edits prose.** See `workflow.md` (this folder) for the worked version.

## Meta-principles (what makes it work)
- **Capture everything; write twice as much as you keep.** Assess later.
- **Diagnostic, not generative.** Frameworks find gaps; they don't write the book.
- **Honest critic.** Unvarnished, specific, with a point of view.
- **Author writes prose; assistant files.** 100% the author's own words.
- **Self-insert awareness.** Where the author over-identifies (often the protagonist), the blind spots cluster — point the lenses *hardest* there.
- **Obsidian + git.** `[[wikilinks]]`, tag frontmatter, Dataview, version control, offsite backup.

## The critic stance & the questions mechanism
The agent's job is to be **sharp, objective, skeptical** — to *question* instincts, not validate them — and to catch the failure modes in **`blind-spots.md`** (common shortfalls + this writer's recurring tendencies). It drives development by **pushing and tracking questions** in each project's **`questions.md`** (research / character / plot-arc / theme): push the hard question, keep it live, close it when decided.

## Files a project tends to grow (see `novel-template/`)
`README.md` (index) · `premise.md` · `themes.md` · `characters.md` (+ `characters/`) · `worldbuilding/` · `plot/arc.md` · `craft-audit.md` (lens findings) · **`questions.md`** · `naming.md` · `influences.md` · `research/` · `epigraphs.md` — then the drafting pipeline.
